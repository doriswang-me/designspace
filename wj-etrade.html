01 / Overview

The Problem & Context

WJ-Etrade is a B2C platform for premium outdoor furniture covers. Since 2023, sales declined — data revealed a high drop-off from search to checkout, suggesting users struggled to discover products and understand their value.

As the sole UX Analyst & Designer, I collaborated closely with the PM to redesign the layout, introduce a product customization page, and increase both engagement and conversion rates.

Core Problem
50+ searches → 0 purchases
Users hit dead-ends repeatedly, abandoning sessions before ever converting.
User Pain
Can't find what they need
Poor discoverability, unclear product info, no guided customization path.
Business Pain
Low conversion & retention
Cart abandonment and low repeat purchase rate threatening revenue growth.

03 / Competitor Analysis

Understanding the Landscape

I benchmarked WJ-Etrade against three direct competitors — Wayfair, Coverstone, and Classic Accessories — across five key UX criteria to identify where WJ-Etrade fell behind and where it had an edge.

Competitive audit across Navigation & Filtering, PDP quality, Checkout Flow, Trust & Reviews, and Mobile Experience
Gap 1 — Navigation
Missing dimension-based filtering
All 3 competitors allow users to filter by furniture type and dimensions. WJ-Etrade had none — forcing manual browsing through long, unstructured product lists.
Gap 2 — Trust & Reviews
No material trust signals
Coverstone and Classic Accessories surface waterproof ratings, UV specs, and photo reviews prominently. WJ-Etrade relied on brand reputation alone.
Strength — Checkout
Checkout flow already solid
WJ-Etrade's checkout flow matched Wayfair's standard — confirming the friction lived upstream in discovery and product detail, not at payment.
04 / Returning Customer Interviews

What Users Actually Said

I conducted remote interviews with returning customers focused on the repeat purchase flow — understanding why they returned, what they trusted, and where the current experience still created friction around fit, reordering, and confidence.

Insight 1 — Returning customers want to quickly understand differences between product types without manual comparison
Insight 2 & 3 — Material trust signals and customization flexibility surfaced as critical unmet needs
05 / Insight Synthesis

Turning Research into Direction

How Might We
"How might we streamline the shopping experience to improve user satisfaction and boost both purchase and return customer rates?"
Synthesis 01 — Category-based navigation structure reduces cognitive load by letting users self-identify furniture type instantly
Synthesis 02 — Making material quality and trust signals explicitly visible throughout the browsing and product detail experience
06 / Design

From Wireframes to Final Design

I produced 80+ wireframes across all major page types, developed a content model with the copywriter and PM, and iterated through multiple rounds based on stakeholder feedback and usability patterns.

PRINCIPLE 01
Characteristics First, Promotions Second
Lead with product quality before promotional messaging — builds trust with first-time visitors and justifies premium pricing.
PRINCIPLE 02
Category-Driven Navigation
Reorganize discovery around furniture type and cover function — reducing cognitive load and time-to-relevant-product.
PRINCIPLE 03
Personalization as a Feature
Make customization a prominent, guided experience — a dedicated page with clear entry points throughout the journey.
Highlight 1 — Collection Page
Product slideshow with 6 cover categories
Redesigned collection page surfaces 4 key product attributes, organizes 6 furniture-type categories, and adds a clear value proposition — reducing decision friction significantly.
Highlight 2 — Product Detail Page
Material selector + dimension guide
Rebuilt PDP with material comparison, dimension guide, lifestyle imagery, and a persistent "Add to Cart" CTA — addressing the main trust and friction gaps from research.
Highlight 3 — Custom Order Page (New)
A guided customization experience — entirely new to the platform
The redesigned site places strong emphasis on customization, with a dedicated page providing clear step-by-step guidance for initiating a custom order. This was absent from the previous site and directly addressed the key need surfaced in customer interviews.

08 / Takeaway

What I Learned

🎯
Proficiency in User-Centric Design
Translating complex product advantages into user-friendly content that clearly communicates distinctive value — good design reduces cognitive load at every decision point.
🤝

Ensuring UX work is aligned with overarching business goals — through close collaboration with PM and copywriter — was essential to shipping successfully and defensibly.
